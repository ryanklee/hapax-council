## Why this is refused

### NIH policy 2025-09: AI-developed-application ban

In September 2025, NIH issued a policy explicitly prohibiting
**AI-developed applications**: research-grant applications where
the proposal text, study design, or deliverable infrastructure is
generated by AI systems. Hapax is constitutionally AI-developed —
every artifact bears Hapax + Claude Code co-authorship per the
operator's constitutional posture (`feedback_co_publishing_auto_only_unsettled_contribution`,
2026-04-25).

Submitting a Hapax-derived NIH application would violate NIH's
eligibility policy. Eligibility misrepresentation would violate
NIH ToS + Hapax's own axiom governance (the
`management_governance` axiom + axiom-precedent integrity).

### Refusal-as-data: the eligibility ineligibility is the artefact

This refusal is **itself citable evidence** for the academic-
spectacle strategy: "Hapax cannot apply to NIH because NIH bans
AI-developed applications; the refusal is constitutive of the
AI-developed-app status." The refusal is not a workaround obstacle
— it's a load-bearing data point in the broader argument that AI-
developed research infrastructure exists in a different regulatory
landscape than human-authored research infrastructure.
